Poker

Why it's interesting

Because it's fun, it gives emotions and at the same time it's a game of a skill.

Which poker?

The below applies to Texas Holdem.

Number of players and chances

The more people are in game the less chances you have (even with AA).

Probability to have a specific starting hand

  • AA: 0.5%.
  • AA or KK: 1%.
  • AK: 0.9%.
  • Any pocket pair: 5.9%.
  • Any single ace: 15%.
  • Two suited cards: 23.5%.

Preflop one-on-one chances to win

  • Larger pocket pair (AA) vs smaller pocket pair (KK) or any other hand: 80%.
  • Pocket pair (JJ) vs one overcard (AT): 66%.
  • Pocket pair (JJ) vs overcards (QK): 52% (literally it's a coin flip).
  • Overcards (AK) vs undercards (JT): 57%.
  • One overcard (A2 vs 89): 50% (another coin flip).
  • Better kicker (AK vs AQ): 70%.

From the flop to the turn

  • Four cards to a flush: 19%.
  • Open ended straight (NOT gutshot): 18%.
  • Set to a full house or four of a kind: 15%.
  • Two pair to a full house: 8%.

From the flop by the river

  • Open-ended straight flush draw to complete at least a straight: 54%.
  • Flash draw to a flush: 35%.
  • Set to at least a full house: 33%.
  • Open ended straight draw to complete: 32%.
  • Inside (gutshot) straight draw to complete: 17%.
  • Two pair to at least a full house: 17%.
  • One pair to at least a set: 8%.
  • Open-ended straight flush draw to complete: 8%.

x4/x2 rule:

  1. Percent of chances to win on flop: number of outs x 4.
  2. Percent of chances to win on turn: number of outs x 2.
