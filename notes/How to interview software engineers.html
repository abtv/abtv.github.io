How to interview software engineers

Define your goal as an employer

Write down what you expect from a new employee. Everything else is nice but usually not relevant.

Ask open questions

It is better to ask open questions than closed ones, at least for middle-level positions and higher: you will see how many aspects your candidate may reason about and you can always ask specific questions when needed.

Examples of open questions

1. Technical open questions

You goal here is to assess breadth and depth of candidate's technical skills. Remember: you need to match it to your job requirements.

  • When to use and when to avoid michroservice architecture?
  • What was the most interesting project you was working on?
  • What was the most challenging task you solved recently?
  • Your manager asks you to help with a legacy code with many bugs. What are you going to do?

2. Soft-skills open questions

You goal here is to understand how your candidate works in a team.

  • You are working on a task and you realise it's much harder than you expected. What you are going to do about it?
  • You need to learn something new. What will you do?
  • Our important client complains that our application works slow and your manager asks you to help. What you are going to do?
