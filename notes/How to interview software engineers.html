How to interview software engineers

Checklist

1. Define your goal as an employer

Write down what you expect from a new employee for this particular role.

For example, I may like to see:

  1. Experience in distributed systems:
  • If we use relational databases I will look for experience with optimistic and pessimistic locking.
  • If we use microservices and asynchronous events I will look for experience with logic implemented in an idempotent way.
  1. Communication skills:
  • Ability and desire to manage a working conflict and come to a solution.
  • Ability and desire to push back when necessary.
  • Strive for team results over individual results.

Important: pay attention only to job-required skills. Prepare a plan what you are going to ask.

2. Study resume

  • Analyse logic behind a career track of a candidate.
  • Analyse what the candidate interested in and how deep his knowledge in the area.
  • Analyse how the candidate studies new things.
  • Analyse if complexity of their projects was rising.

3. Interview

3.1. Introduce yourself, your company, and your team

Make sure we are on the same page.

3.2. Describe how you are going to structure the interview

This will help you both to know what to expect and when.

3.3. Ask questions

The assessment of skills.

3.4. Give a candidate time to ask you questions

Interview is a mutual process.

4. Write down notes on the candidate

This is not just go/no go, we need to write a small note on each required skill. Later it will help to compare candidates if we have several of them.

Important details

Ask open questions

It is better to ask open questions than closed ones, at least for middle-level positions and higher: you will see how many aspects your candidate may reason about and you can always ask specific questions when needed.

Examples of open questions

1. Technical open questions

You goal here is to assess breadth and depth of candidate's technical skills. Remember: you need to match it to your job requirements.

  • When to use and when to avoid michroservice architecture?
  • What was the most interesting project you was working on?
  • What was the most challenging task you solved recently?
  • Your manager asks you to help with a legacy code with many bugs. What are you going to do?

2. Communication open questions

You goal here is to understand how your candidate works in a team.

  • You are working on a task and you realise it's much harder than you expected. What you are going to do about it?
  • You need to learn something new. What will you do?
  • Our important client complains that our application works slow and your manager asks you to help. What you are going to do?
