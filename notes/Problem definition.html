Problem definition

These rules and questions are taken from a book called “Are your lights on?”.

Rules:

  1. Don’t rush to solve the problem!
  2. Don’t take their solution method for the problem definition!
  3. If I can't think of at least three things that may be wrong with my understanding of the problem then I don't understand the problem.
  4. Test your definition on a foreigner or a child. Try to find a misfit.
  5. Don’t solve problems for other people if they can do it themselves.

Questions:

  1. What is a problem?
  • Problem is the difference between things as desired and things as perceived.
  • What do I desire and perceive?
  1. Am I the source of the problem?
  • Sanity check
  1. Who has the problem?
  • What are the parties involved?
  • What do they want separately?
  1. Do they really want to solve the problem?
  2. Is it ethical to solve their problems?
  3. Can we find a misfit if we take a different point of view?
  4. Can I change the problem statement to make it different?
  5. What other problems will appear when I solve this problem?
  • There are always consequences, name them!
